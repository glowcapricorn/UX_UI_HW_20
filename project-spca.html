Introduction

In this project, we will explore how the user experience (UX) design of Ontario SPCA pet adoption website can be improved to increase user engagement and adoption rates. The objective of this project is to understand the pain points and user needs during the adoption process and create a design that makes it easy and enjoyable for users to find and adopt their perfect pet. Through a user-centered approach, we will identify key areas of improvement and propose design solutions to address them.

Research

The research conducted:

  • 1. User Interview
  • 2. Stakeholder Interview
  • 3. Competitor Analysis
  • 4. Empathy Map
  • 5. User Persona

Problem Statement

Millions of animals are currently in shelter/rescue care centers, waiting to be adopted. How might we raise awareness regarding “adopt, don't shop” philosophy?

Iteration

Sketch

The initial sketching out ideas to visualize how the user interface (UI) will look and function, and identify potential usability issues or areas for improvement early on in the design process.

Usability Testing

Usability testing is done by observing users interacting with our design and gathering feedback on their experience. By analyzing the data collected, we identified areas where the design can be improved.
